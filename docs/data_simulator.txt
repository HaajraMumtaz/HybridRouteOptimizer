You are a data engine generating a synthetic machine learning dataset for training a CatBoost regression model.

**GOAL**
Predict `congestion_multiplier` — a float representing how much slower than free-flow a road segment is, given zone, road type, time, and conditions. This multiplier is applied as: `final_travel_time = (distance_km / speed_limit_kmh) * 60 * congestion_multiplier`

Typical range: 0.6 (empty night highway) to 3.5 (Friday evening peak, inner city Lahore).

---

**CONTEXT**
This dataset represents road traffic behavior in Lahore, Pakistan. Lahore has 23 defined zones. Each row represents one road segment observation under specific temporal and environmental conditions.

**MANDATORY CONGESTION RULES — ALL MUST BE RESPECTED**

Road type base multipliers (clear weather, midday, weekday):
```
highway:      0.9–1.1
main_road:    1.1–1.4
street:       1.3–1.8
service_lane: 1.4–2.0
```

Zone personality — apply as additive bias on top of road base:
```
zones 1, 21 (Androon, Anarkali):     +0.4 to +0.8 always — permanently congested, narrow streets
zones 5, 10 (DHA):                   +0.0 to +0.3 midday, +0.6 to +1.2 evening_peak
zones 8, 9 (Gulberg/Cantt):          +0.3 to +0.6 morning_peak, +0.8 to +1.4 friday midday
zones 11, 14, 15 (Raiwind, Suburbs, Lakecity): -0.1 to +0.2 — sparse, near free-flow
zones 17, 18 (Barki):                +0.0 to +0.2 — low density
zones 6, 23 (Ferozepur Spine, Canal Road): +0.3 to +0.7 all day — arterial roads always busy
zone 20 (Shahdara):                  +0.2 to +0.5 — moderate
zones 3, 4 (Bahria, Valencia):       +0.1 to +0.4 — suburban, moderate
```

Time slot multipliers (applied on top of zone+road base):
```
morning_peak:  × 1.3–1.6
midday:        × 1.0–1.2
evening_peak:  × 1.4–1.9
night:         × 0.5–0.7
```

Day type rules:
```
friday:  morning_peak × 1.1, midday × 1.4 (Jummah effect zones 1,8,21), evening_peak × 1.6
weekend: all slots × 0.85 except zones 1, 21 which stay high
holiday: treat as weekend × 0.9, except zones 1, 21, 6 which stay at weekday levels
```

Weather multipliers (multiplicative):
```
clear:      × 1.0
cloudy:     × 1.05
rain:       × 1.2–1.35
heavy_rain: × 1.4–1.6
fog:        × 1.25–1.45
```

Fog is more common in zones 11, 14, 15 (southern/rural). Heavy rain hits zones 1, 21, 6 hardest (poor drainage).

Physical feature effects:
```
road_curvature > 1.3:  × 1.1–1.2 (winding roads slow down)
has_signal = 1:        +0.1 to +0.3
is_construction = 1:   × 1.2–1.4
num_lanes:             inverse effect — more lanes = lower multiplier, especially highways
is_one_way = 1:        slight reduction -0.05 to -0.1 (no opposing traffic conflicts)
```

---

**INTERACTION EFFECTS — MANDATORY NON-LINEARITY**

These must be present in the data, not as separate columns but reflected in the target:

- `heavy_rain × evening_peak × street` in zones 1, 6, 21 → multiplier can reach 3.0–3.5
- `friday × midday × has_signal=1` in zones 8, 9 → +0.4 extra on top
- `highway × night × clear` → can go as low as 0.55–0.65
- `is_construction × morning_peak × main_road` → ×1.5 effect, not just ×1.2
- `is_holiday × night` → lowest possible multipliers, 0.55–0.70 across all zones
- `weekend × zones(1,21) × evening_peak` → stays high at 1.8–2.2 (tourist/leisure traffic)

```
road_type distribution: highway 15%, main_road 40%, street 35%, service_lane 10%
time_slot: roughly equal split across 4 slots

weather: clear 55%, cloudy 20%, rain 12%, heavy_rain 5%, fog 8%

```
